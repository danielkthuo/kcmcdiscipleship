Topic 1: Prayer

ACTS Prayer Method

ACTS is a simple pattern to follow when praying ‚Äî helping us cover all parts of our relationship with God.

Prayer is much more than asking for things ‚Äî it's a heart-to-heart conversation with God. It includes:

  • Praising God
  • Confessing our sins
  • Giving thanks
  • Asking for help

God wants to hear from us ‚Äî not because He needs information, but because He desires relationship.

Topic 2: Fellowship

Fellowship is the friendship and spiritual connection we share with other believers as we grow in Jesus together.

Hebrews 10:24-25 - "Let us not give up meeting together... but encourage one another."

What is Fellowship?

  • Fellowship with God ‚Äì our personal relationship with Him through Jesus
  • Fellowship with others ‚Äì walking in love, unity, and purpose with fellow believers
Importance of Fellowship

1. We All Grow Together

Just as flowers grow better together in a garden than alone, we can grow in our faith more with others who also love Jesus.

Proverbs 27:17 - "Iron sharpens iron, so one person sharpens another."

2. We Help Each Other

When one friend is sad or struggling, other friends can pray with them, listen to them, and encourage them. We share joy and hard times with each other.

Galatians 6:2 - "Bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the law of Christ."

3. We Learn From Each Other

We listen to multiple tellings of the Bible stories, sing songs, participate in activities, and learn how to follow Jesus together. Each time we gather, we share what we've learned, encourage one another, and grow stronger in our walk with Christ.

üìñ Colossians 3:16 ‚Äî "Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly in all wisdom, teaching and admonishing one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing with grace in your hearts to the Lord."

4. We Are All One Family

God calls the church His family. We belong to God and we belong to each other.

1 Corinthians 12:27 - "Now you are the body of Christ, and members individually."

5. Jesus Is with Us

Jesus promises that He will be with us when we meet in His name.

Matthew 18:20 - "For where two or three gather in my name, there am I with them."

Accountability in Fellowship

Galatians 6:1-2 - "Brothers and sisters, if someone is caught in a sin, you who live by the Spirit should restore that person gently. But watch yourselves, or you also may be tempted. Carry each other's burdens, and in this way, you will fulfill the law of Christ."

What is Accountability?

"Accountability means helping each other stay close to God and grow in faith. It means checking in, correcting with love, and encouraging each other when we stumble."

Key Principles

  • Gentleness: "Restore that person gently" - never harsh, embarrassing, or forceful
  • Burden-bearing: Praying with friends who are struggling, listening without gossip, helping make better decisions
  • Humility: "Watch yourselves" - be careful not to fall into the same sin while helping others
  • Same-Gender Accountability: Important for creating safe boundaries and avoiding distractions

Activity: Accountability Partners

Form accountability pairs or triads (groups of 2-3 learners) within the same gender.

Ways to practice:

  • Choose same-gender partners for prayer and check-ins
  • Check in on each other spiritually - ask how Bible reading is going, share prayer requests
  • Practice respect, confidentiality, and encouraging words
Serving Together

1 Peter 4:10 (NKJV) - "As each one has received a gift, minister it to one another, as good stewards of the manifold grace of God."

Why Serve Together?

  • Serving is an act of love and obedience - reflecting Christ to others
  • Fellowship includes working side-by-side to bless others
  • Serving deepens friendships and builds unity
  • Everyone has unique gifts to contribute

Service Project Ideas

  • Visiting the sick or elderly
  • Cleaning the church or helping with Sunday School
  • Collecting food or clothing for needy families
  • Writing thank-you notes for pastors, teachers, or parents

Real Story: The Sandwich Team

In a small church youth group in Nairobi, the idea of community service was born in simple terms. Every Saturday after Bible study, the kids would pool their money to buy bread, peanut butter, and bananas.

They made 20 sandwiches, put them in paper bags, and walked right down to a street corner where they knew some children were living begging for food. A little Bible verse was handed out with each sandwich, and the kids prayed with anyone who wanted prayer.

Initially, it seemed so small and simple, but gradually, the community took notice. One of the street boys has started coming to church, and another went back to school upon encouragement and assistance offered. The youth group became famous for kindness, and more kids evidenced their commitment to being engaged.

Topic 3: Clear Understanding of the Gospel

Objective: To ensure learners grasp what the Gospel is and how it impacts their daily life.

Visual representation of the separation caused by sin and how Jesus bridges the gap between God and humanity.

1 The Problem

Sin creates a large gap between God and us. It separates us from Him.

We can't close this gap by ourselves, no matter how many good things we do.

Romans 3:23 - "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."

2 The Solution

The good news is that Jesus' cross forms a bridge over this gap.

Romans 5:8 - "But God shows His love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."

God's love is so great, He made a way for us through Jesus' sacrifice.

3 The Way Across

To use this bridge, we must have faith.

John 14:6 - "I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me."

By believing in Him, we can cross over the bridge and have a relationship with God.

John 3:16 ‚Äì The heart of God's love

"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."

Note for Learners: This verse is considered the "heart" of the Gospel because it explains God's immense love for us. God loved humanity so much that He sent Jesus to die for our sins. Believing in Jesus is the only way to receive eternal life and be saved from perishing. This verse emphasizes that salvation is a gift available to everyone, no matter who they are.

Romans 10:9-10 ‚Äì Belief and confession

"If you declare with your mouth, 'Jesus is Lord,' and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved. For it is with your heart that you believe and are justified, and it is with your mouth that you profess your faith and are saved."

Note for Learners: Salvation comes through faith in Jesus Christ. These verses highlight the importance of belief in Jesus' death and resurrection, and the confession of Him as Lord. This is the process of salvation: you believe in your heart and declare it with your mouth. It's not just about words‚Äîit's about a genuine heart change.

Ephesians 2:8-9 ‚Äì Salvation is a gift

"For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith‚Äîand this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God‚Äî not by works, so that no one can boast."

Note for Learners: This verse teaches that salvation is a gift from God, not something we can earn by being good or doing good works. It's God's grace‚ÄîHis unmerited favor toward us‚Äîthat saves us. This verse helps us understand that no one can boast about their salvation, because it's not something we deserve or can achieve on our own. It's a pure gift that we receive by faith.

2 Corinthians 5:21 ‚Äì Jesus became sin for us

"God made Him who had no sin to be sin for us, so that in Him we might become the righteousness of God."

Note for Learners: This verse explains the great exchange that happened at the cross: Jesus, who was sinless, took on the penalty for our sin. Because of His sacrifice, we are made righteous before God. This means that Jesus bore our sins so we could be forgiven and stand before God as clean and righteous. This is one of the foundational truths of Christianity: Jesus died in our place.

Acts 4:12 ‚Äì Salvation is found in no one else

"Salvation is found in no one else, for there is no other name under heaven given to mankind by which we must be saved."

Note for Learners: This verse clearly states that Jesus is the only way to salvation. There are no other paths to God except through faith in Jesus Christ. This verse is critical because it emphasizes the exclusivity of Christ‚Äîthere's no other person or religion that can save us. Jesus Himself is the one who bridges the gap between us and God.

How You Relate to Yourself: The Center Hub and Outer Rim

1. CHRIST (The Hub)

When Christ is at the center of your life, your identity and purpose change. You no longer live for yourself but for Him. He gives you a new heart and a new way of living.

Galatians 2:20 - "I have been crucified with Christ, and I no longer live, but Christ lives in me..."

2 Corinthians 5:17 - "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come..."

Note for Learners: When Christ is at the center of your life, your identity and purpose change. You no longer live for yourself but for Him. He gives you a new heart and a new way of living.

Ask yourself: Is Christ the center of your decisions, relationships, and dreams?

Facilitator Tip: Help learners reflect on practical ways to keep Jesus central ‚Äî daily devotion, obedience, and trusting Him in every part of life.

2. THE OBEDIENT CHRISTIAN IN ACTION (The Rim)

Some acts of obedience to God are internal, like our attitudes, thoughts, habits, and motives. These show our love for God and help shape who we are on the inside.

But what's inside us eventually shows on the outside in the way we treat others, speak, and live every day. True obedience is not just about doing the right things it's about having a heart that wants to please God.

John 14:21 (NKJV) - "He who has My commandments and keeps them, it is he who loves Me. And he who loves Me will be loved by My Father, and I will love him and manifest Myself to him."

Romans 12:1 (NKJV) - "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, which is your reasonable service."

Note for Learners: Obedience is the outer rim that keeps the wheel moving forward. Without obedience, all the other spokes become weak. Obedience shows that your faith is real.

Start obeying in the small things: honesty, kindness, forgiving others.

Facilitator Tip: Share testimonies of simple obedience and its fruit. Emphasize that obedience brings peace, joy, and spiritual momentum.

How You Relate to God: The Vertical Spokes

1. The Word (Bible)

The Bible is God's voice to you. It guides your life, strengthens your faith, and corrects your path. The more you read and apply it, the more you grow.

God uses His word to speak directly to us, revealing not only who He is, but how He calls us to live and interact with everyone around us. Personal intake of God's Word is essential for our spiritual health and growth.

Joshua 1:8 - "Keep this Book of the Law always on your lips; meditate on it day and night..."

2 Timothy 3:16 - "All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness..."

Note for Learners: The Bible is God's voice to you. It guides your life, strengthens your faith, and corrects your path. The more you read and apply it, the more you grow.

Start small: a verse a day with reflection. Don't rush, let it sink in.

Facilitator Tip: Encourage the use of reading plans or simple Bible tools. Demonstrate how to journal or highlight key lessons from Scripture.

2. Prayer

Prayer is talking to God‚Äîanytime, anywhere. It's how we express our heart to Him, ask for help, and listen for His guidance.

Prayer not only trains our hearts and minds to know the power and glory of God, but also turns His ear towards action in our lives.

Philippians 4:6-7 - "Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer... present your requests to God..."

1 Thessalonians 5:17 - "Pray without ceasing."

Note for Learners: Prayer is talking to God‚Äîanytime, anywhere. It's how we express our heart to Him, ask for help, and listen for His guidance.

Remember: God isn't looking for perfect words. Just honesty and consistency.

Facilitator Tip: Introduce the ACTS method (Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication). Practice short group prayers to build confidence.

How You Relate to Others: The Horizontal Spokes

1. Fellowship

God made us to grow together, not alone. The Christian life was not meant to be lived in isolation. God wants us to build each other up, love each other, and make each other strong in our faith.

Every time we gather together ‚Äî for church, small groups, or discipleship classes ‚Äî we experience God's presence in a unique way and actively encourage one another through prayer, support, and worship.

Hebrews 10:24-25 - "Let us not give up meeting together... but encourage one another..."

Acts 2:42 - "They devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and to fellowship..."

Note for Learners: Fellowship helps us grow together, not just alone. We encourage, correct, and carry each other in faith. Christianity is not a solo walk‚Äîit's a family journey.

Next step: Plug into a church group or Bible study. Find spiritual friends.

Facilitator Tip: Encourage group sharing. Plan fun and spiritual bonding activities like service projects, games, or prayer partners.

2. Witnessing

God has placed the privilege and duty upon believers to proclaim to the world about the good news of Christ's work on Earth (Matthew 4:19). Furthermore, it is the natural overflow of a rich and meaningful life in Christ to be sharing about His magnificent grace (Romans 1:16).

Matthew 28:19-20 - "Go and make disciples of all nations..."

Romans 1:16 - "I am not ashamed of the gospel, because it is the power of God that brings salvation..."

Note for Learners: Witnessing is simply sharing your faith ‚Äî what Jesus has done for you. It's about being light to others through words and actions.

Remember: You don't need to preach. Just share your story and God's love.

Facilitator Tip: Practice a short testimony with learners. Teach simple evangelism tools (e.g., The Bridge illustration or Romans Road, Evangecube).

Summary

Summary for Learners

The Wheel Illustration teaches that a balanced spiritual life keeps you growing strong in Jesus. If one spoke is weak, your spiritual growth is affected.

Let Christ be the center, and keep the wheel turning through the Word, prayer, fellowship, witnessing, and obedience.
