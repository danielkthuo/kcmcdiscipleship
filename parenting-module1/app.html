Monthly Goal for Parents:

üìñ SESSION 1 - ATMOSPHERE IS IMPORTANT TO GOD

‚ñº

THEME: The womb is aware of what surrounds it

KEY SCRIPTURE: Deuteronomy 6:6-7a

OBJECTIVE:

To help parents identify that the spiritual and emotional atmospheres they present represent who they are becoming and what their child understands about identity and experience.

TEACHING POINTS:

  • Babies experience emotional tone (voice, stress, calm).
    • Even from the womb, babies can feel the emotional state of the mother and surrounding voices.
    • Calm, prayerful, joyful laughing and worship creates security and stability for the developing child.
    • Constant tension, anxiety or conflict potentially leaves a seed of fear or stress in the early childhood experience.
  • A godly atmosphere allows children to attach securely to truth and love.
    • When a child's earliest experiences are filled with peace, Scripture, and love, they grow immersed in a sense of security, both emotionally and spiritually.
    • A faith-filled atmosphere becomes the norm and enables them to lean into trust in others and in God as they grow.
  • You are shaping the child's earliest understanding of God and love.
    • You are shaping far more than the child's emergent brain and body; you are also shaping a worldview.
    • The environment you create now will be your child's first association to security, belonging and identity.
  • Even businesses dedicate their space to God‚Äîhow much more your womb, and home?
    • Many Christian-owned businesses actually set apart and dedicate their buildings, offices, and environments to God.
    • Your womb is the child's first home, and your house is the child's first classroom and first sanctuary. Each day invite God into your space.

MONTH 4: JOY IN THE WOMB

THEME: "My Baby Responds to Praise and Joy"

CORE SCRIPTURE: Luke 1:44 (NKJV)

üìñ SESSION 1 - JOY AWAKENS THE WOMB

‚ñº

THEME: Your baby can experience joy through the Spirit

KEY VERSE: Luke 1:44

OBJECTIVE:

Awaken parents to the need to identify the spiritual responsiveness of their unborn child and purposefully stir joy through praise and worship.

TEACHING POINTS:

  • Babies respond in the spiritual realm to God's presence.
    • Luke 1:44 states that John the Baptist leaped for joy in the womb as Mary, pregnant with Jesus spoke.
    • This was not simply a random kick, this was John spiritually responding to the presence of the Messiah.
    • Your baby is not spiritually asleep; they can feel and respond to spiritual stimuli.
  • Worship can stir joy in the womb.
    • Singing, praying, and playing worship music can spark joy in your baby's spirit. Joy is not just an emotion, but a fruit of the spirit (Gal. 5:22) and while it may not fully manifest in your child's personality until they are outside and much older, it can still begin to be formed before birth.
    • What is experienced in the womb becomes the first faith language of the child.
  • Your praise today becomes your child's atmosphere of joy.
    • A parent who worships creates an atmosphere of joy. The praise with which your child's spirit becomes familiar does not just praise God, it brings peace and safety and love to the child. Thus, your child will associate praise with peace and safety.
    • Praise shapes the child's earliest emotional and spiritual identity.

  • The presence of God brings fullness of joy
    • Psalm 16:11 reminds us that joy doesn't come from our context, but in His presence.
    • When we praise, we invite his presence in your living room, your womb, your nursery becomes a sanctuary of joy. This breeds peace. When you value presence over performance, your family becomes a family of peace and joy.
  • Homes that are joy-filled are praise-filled homes
    • Praise is caught more than taught. As your child observes this in action, praise becomes a part of their life.
    • When homes are filled with singing, reading Scripture and rejoicing in the Lord's words, children gravitate toward joy, especially spiritually. If you develop a rhythm of praise, it will also help you become resilient parents!

üìñ SESSION 3 - EMOTIONAL HEALTH FOR PARENTS

‚ñº

THEME: Managing your emotions creates the inner world for your baby.

KEY VERSE: Proverbs 17:22 (NKJV)

"A merry heart does good, like medicine, but a broken spirit dries the bones."

GOAL:

To help parents consider emotional health to maintain a joyful womb environment.

TEACHING POINTS:

  • The state of one's emotions can have an impact on the development of your baby.
    • Scientific studies have shown that a mother's stress, calm, or fears can shape fetal development, hormone levels, and brain chemistry.
    • The womb is your baby's first "emotional class." Your emotional habits create the climate of safety, joy or peace during your baby's earliest experiences.
  • The wise man says, "A cheerful heart is a good medicine".
    • A happy heart is medicine‚Äîfor you and your child. God's wisdom from Proverbs 17:22 explains joy is healing, restorative, and energizing.
    • A joyful inner life helps you be more emotionally aware of your own state and more spiritually engaged as a parent. And God invites us to cultivate a joyful heart, not just for ourselves, but for the baby we bear.
  • Daily joy habits create, in the long term, building inner resilience.
    • Just as physical muscles grow stronger through repeated, daily demands, our emotional and spiritual muscles also develop through a daily regimen. Keeping a joy journal, creating a habit of giving thanks, going for prayer walks, singing, and meditating on God's promises all create healthy and joyful ways of thinking in your mind and spirit.
    • These joyful habits sustain you through hard days and help you return to the womb as a place of rest rather than a place of emotional reaction.

    • Your unborn child is already being trained in atmosphere; what kind of spirit are you brooding?

TRIMESTER 1: RECOVERING FROM FIRST TRIMESTER TRAUMA

LESSON TOPIC: "God Sees Me in My Pain"

SCRIPTURE: Genesis 16:13 (NKJV)

"Then she called the name of the Lord who spoke to her, You-Are-the-God-Who-Sees; for she said, 'Have I also here seen Him who sees me?'"

üìñ LESSON OVERVIEW

Many women experience trauma during the first trimester of pregnancy - from surprise pregnancies to miscarriages, rejection, medical complications, and fears- and many women endure trauma in silence. This lesson provides an opportunity for pregnant caregivers to show up in the sacred space, which is safe, to heal and be affirmed in the presence of God. We consider the story of Hagar, a woman alone, afraid, and pregnant, and yet seen by God.

üéØ LEARNING OBJECTIVES

At the end of the lesson, participants will:

  • Understand the emotional and spiritual effects of trauma at the beginning of pregnancy.
  • Grasp from Hagar's story that God sees and cares for us in places of pain.
  • Be equipped with spiritual and emotional tools for processing trauma.
  • Begin the process of personalized healing through journaling, meditation on scripture, and prayer.

üß† UNDERSTANDING TRAUMA

Trauma defined: An overwhelming emotional response to deeply distressing events.

Examples of trauma in early pregnancy:

  • A history of miscarriage
  • Unplanned/unwanted pregnancy
  • Intimate partner rejection or abuse/harm
  • Medical emergencies
  • Shame or isolation
  • PTSD from sexual abuse

How Do I Know I Have Trauma?

Many people undergo trauma at some level and are not aware of it, particularly in early pregnancy where there can be intense emotions, hormonal fluctuations, and physical changes. Trauma is more than just what happened to you; it is about how it impacted you.

Here are signs that you may be carrying unhealed trauma from your current or previous pregnancy:

Category Sign/Symptom Description
Emotional Unexplained crying Frequent tears or emotional outbursts without a clear trigger.
Intense sadness or hopelessness Feeling like life has lost meaning or that you can't imagine a joyful future.
Irritability or anger Snapping easily at others, feeling emotionally overwhelmed.
Anxiety or panic Constant worrying about your health, baby's survival, or fear of something bad happening.
Guilt or shame Thoughts like "It's my fault," "I don't deserve this baby," or feeling ashamed of your feelings.
Cognitive (Thoughts) Racing thoughts Can't stop thinking or replaying bad memories in your mind.
Flashbacks Sudden emotional or physical re-experiencing of past trauma.
Intrusive thoughts Unwanted scary or negative thoughts about pregnancy or birth.
Difficulty concentrating Trouble focusing, reading, or completing tasks.
Memory gaps Struggling to recall parts of the trauma or even parts of the day.

üïäÔ∏è GUIDED HEALING PRACTICE

Healing Practice 1: Writing a Lament to God

God is not afraid of your naked honesty. Tell Him. Write to Him, tell Him what hurts.

Journal Prompts (write in silence):

  • "God, I didn't expect to feel..."
  • "Right now, I am afraid because..."
  • "I need for you to say...."

Healing Practice 2: Scripture Meditation & Breath

Scripture: Psalm 34:18 - "The Lord is near to the brokenhearted..."

Breath Prayer:

  • Inhale: "The Lord is near..."
  • Exhale: "...to the broken-hearted."

Repeat 5 times very slowly.

Healing Practice 3: Group Blessing Prayer

Example of a group prayer- Gather the group into a circle. Speak softly:

"May the God who saw Hagar see you. May He collect your tears, hold your fears, and cover you with His peace. You are not invisible. You are seen. You are safe. You are loved."
